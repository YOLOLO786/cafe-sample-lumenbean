About Lumen Beans

Our process

How our coffee is made — from seed to cup (a close, thoughtful process)

A human-led process built on clarity and craft

At Lumen Beans we treat coffee as both craft and conversation. We begin at origin — building direct relationships with small farms or trusted cooperatives who farm with care. Those relationships matter because they help us pick varieties and lots that deliver clear, interesting flavors: stone fruit, cocoa, toasted almond or bright citrus depending on season.

When green beans arrive in our micro-roastery they go through an immediate quality check: we sample, smell, and scan for defects, and separate lots meant for espresso, filter, or blends. Our roasting philosophy is intentionally slow and small-batch. We roast to highlight origin character rather than cover it up — lower development, attentive first crack listening, and resting beans overnight before cupping. Each lot has a roast profile saved and continuously tuned based on sensory feedback.

Making coffee for service is equally deliberate. Espresso recipes are pulled with a consistent dosing and yield protocol (18g in → 36g out is a common starting point), timed in the 25–35 second range depending on the shot. We temperature-stabilize group heads, calibrate grinders multiple times per day, and train baristas on milk gymnastics: microfoam that stretches and thins to a velvet texture without scalding (we keep milk below 60°C). For batch brews and filter coffee, we use precise bloom and pour techniques, controlling water temperature and draw time. Cold brew is an 18-hour immersion to produce low acidity and syrupy sweetness.

We also pay attention to the non-coffee details: water quality (we filter and adjust mineral content), fresh grinders (daily burr checks and doser calibration), and even glassware — the way a cup cools affects perception. Finally, we document every change: roast curve, extraction time, grind setting, and customer feedback. That discipline is why customers taste a reliably great cup every visit.

Ultimately the goal is simple: make coffee that invites a pause. Each recipe, from single-origin pour to a house latte, is the result of dozens of tiny choices that together produce something memorable and honest.

Signature & seasonal

Our specialty — the drinks and beans we obsess over

Our specialty program is built on two pillars: single-origin clarity and barista creativity. Single-origin coffees rotate monthly, allowing us to highlight different regions and processing styles — washed Ethiopian with jasmine and bergamot, naturally processed Brazilian with chocolate and stone fruit, sometimes a honey-processed Central American lot exhibiting honeyed sweetness and florals. These lots are roasted to clarity: revealing top notes while keeping body and sweetness balanced. We maintain a daily pour-over schedule for single-origin cups because it allows customers to taste the character pure and unblended.

Parallel to the single-origin program is our signature drinks list. These are recipes refined by baristas in service and lab: house Pistachio Latte (real pistachio paste, toasted nut crumble, pinch of sea salt), the “Late Night Affogato” (a bold double shot over house vanilla bean gelato), and the Citrus Cold Brew (cold brew infused briefly with orange peel and a dash of aromatic bitters). Each signature is tested for balance, mouthfeel, and repeatability — the kind of drink someone orders when they want a memory, not just caffeine.

For espresso we emphasize a “dialed-in” approach — matching roast level and grind so an espresso recipe reliably produces sweetness, low bitterness, and a clean finish across multiple machines and baristas. We publish the day’s espresso origin at the bar, with tasting notes and a recommended milk pairing. That transparency is a specialty in itself: we want customers to know what they’re tasting and why it matters.

Our seasonal roaster bundles are another specialty: small 250g bags roasted and labeled with suggested brewing methods, tasting notes, and a recommended extraction range. They make perfect gifts and allow fans to brew our coffees at home with the guidance they need to reproduce café flavor. Finally, we host monthly cuppings and educational nights where we demonstrate brewing technique, grinder calibration, and basic tasting vocabulary — part of our belief that specialty coffee should be shared, not hoarded.

Every specialty item, from the single-origin pour to the layered seasonal latte, is a conversation starter — it invites curiosity and rewards repeat visits with new discoveries.

Why we stand out

Our features — what makes Lumen Beans different (detailed)

Small-batch Roasting

We roast in small batches to maximize freshness and highlight each lot's unique flavor. Small runs let us tune temperature and time precisely, avoiding one-size-fits-all profiles. This care results in cups that are clean, lively, and traceable back to origin.

Barista Craft & Training

Our team trains weekly on extraction, milk technique, and customer service. We share tasting notes and hold calibration sessions to keep standards consistent across shifts—so each cup meets the same high bar.

Sourcing & Sustainability

We prioritize direct-trade partners and pay premiums for quality and sustainable practices. Transparency matters: we publish the origin of our espresso and rotate seasonal beans to support diverse growers.

A long-form look at the features we focus on — for curious customers

(long-form narrative — 350+ words) At Lumen Beans we intentionally design our space and systems around the idea that good coffee is the result of consistent, considered work. That starts with infrastructure: we invest in quality espresso machines and calibrate them daily, ensure water chemistry is suitable for extraction, and maintain grinders to precise burr alignment so particle distribution stays consistent. On the roasting side we buy green coffees with clarity of profile, and we roast with a philosophy that favors nuance over extremes — a cup should reveal its origin rather than mask it.

In the café, features mean the little conveniences that compound into a superior experience: stable Wi-Fi for remote workers, a selection of power outlets at communal and single tables, strong natural light in the mornings, and comfortable chairs mixed with community tables to accommodate different needs. Our pastries are prepared by a local baker who uses European butter and seasonal fruit; pairing advice is displayed next to the bar so guests can easily match a coffee to a pastry.

Another feature is education: we host regular cuppings and technique nights where customers learn how to taste and how to home-brew. This deepens appreciation and helps customers replicate café-quality results at home. For convenience we offer online ordering via WhatsApp and a simple prepay system for pickup—useful for commuters who want speed without sacrificing quality.

Last, but not least, our commitment to equity and sustainability informs every feature: paying a bit more for traceable lots, reducing single-use waste with composting and encouraging reusable cups with a refill discount. These practices take more time and more attention, but customers tell us they feel the difference in taste and in trust. That combination of technical focus, hospitality, and ethical sourcing is the feature set that defines Lumen Beans.
